When Affiliation with a Cherokee, Choctaw, Chickasaw, Creek, or Seminole Group is claimed:

When affiliation with either the Cherokee, Choctaw, Chickasaw, Creek, or Seminole Tribes is claimed, a search for Indian heritage will be greatly aided by having the name of a person whose name might have appeared on the Final Roll prepared by the Dawes Commission in 1906 (“1906 Final Roll”). To learn information that may aid in the ancestor research please ask for the following information from the Child’s family.

  1. The name of each and every person who is a relative of the Child and who was alive in 1906 and might have been included in the 1906 Final Roll.
  2. The relationship of each named person to the Child.
  3. The date and place of birth of each named person.
  4. The date and place of death of each named person.

(Please attach copies of any documents such as birth certificates, death certificates, baptism certificates, marriage licenses, or certificates, or tribal enrollment or membership documents that may aid in proving the relationship between that relative and the Child).

When Affiliation with a Cherokee Group Located Outside of Oklahoma is claimed:

When affiliation with a Cherokee tribe is claimed, but through any of the states in the South Eastern United States (such as but not limited to: North Carolina, Georgia, or Mississippi) instead of through Oklahoma, a search for Indian heritage will be greatly aided by having the name of a person whose name might appear on the Roll of1924 prepared by U.S. agent Fred Baker under an act of congress (”1942 Baker Roll”). To learn information that may aid in the ancestor research, please ask for the following information from the Child’s family:

  1. The name of each and every person who is a relative of the Child and who was alive in 1906 and might have been included in the 1924 Baker Roll.
  2. The relationship of each named person to the Child.
  3. The date and place of birth of each named person.
  4. The date and place of death of each named person.

(Please attach copies of any documents such as birth certificates, death certificates, baptism certificates, marriage licenses, or certificates, or tribal enrollment or membership documents that may aid in proving the relationship between that relative and the Child).
