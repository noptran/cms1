FETAL ALCOHOL SPECTRUM DISORDER

This section is intended to assist in identifying which children need to be assessed for a Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder (FASD). Reassessment of need for an FASD assessment may need to occur as more information is gathered.

Children who meet at least one of the following criteria will be referred to Clinical RFCA team for staffing regarding diagnostic evaluation. (Check all that apply)

Confirmed Prenatal Alcohol or Drug Exposure

Mother’s self-report of alcohol misuse (more than social) or drug use during pregnancy
Reliable informant reported alcohol misuse or drug use by mother
Child placed in child protective custody at birth due to mother’s alcohol or drug condition
Child’s medical records “indicate child was positive for a substance/high blood alcohol level and detoxed at birth.
Documentation in the child’s chart or a legal record of mother’s history of misuse.
Other

If 3 or more of the identifiers listed below are noted, the individual should be referred to Clinical RFCA team for staffing regarding a diagnostic evaluation.

Height and weight seem small for age
Unusual facial features
Size of head seems small for age
Mental retardation or IQ below familial expectations

Attention problem/impulsive/restless
Learning disability
Speech and/or language delays
Problem with reasoning and judgment
Acts younger than children the same age

DRUG/ALCOHOL

This section is intended to assist in identifying which children need a referral for a drug/alcohol assessment, not to provide a drug/alcohol assessment. The child should be the primary contact for answers to the questions but a caregiver may provide pertinent information if you think the child is in denial/not answering truthfully.

Drug/Alcohol assessment should be recommended if one or more of the following exist: 1) child had an assessment/treatment before and child used in the last thirty (30) days, 2) frequency of use, drug used or amount used in one setting is at a level that would normally cause concern, 3) answers of concern supported by parent or provider.
